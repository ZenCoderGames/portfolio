Game AI: Steering Behaviors

Programming Guide

Steering behaviors are a form of autonomous character navigation in games. Originally developed by Craig Reynolds, these behaviors allow AI characters to navigate environments naturally, avoid obstacles, pursue targets, and exhibit realistic group dynamics.

I made this open source project to share and teach game devs how to use them using Godot as the engine. This implements a simple intuitive architecture to add and remove behaviors to characters that can be easily added to any existing project.

Here is a small demo of Emergent Behavior that can come from the combination of these behaviors. This showcases Flocking, Wander, Obstacle/Wall Avoidance and Fleeing

Supported Behaviors

  • Seek with Arrival - Move towards a target/destination and decelerate to a stop
  • Pursue with Offset - Use velocity tracking to be more accurate and have a defined offset to the target character
  • Flee and Leave - Move away from a target/destination and decelerate based on distance
  • Evade - Use velocity tracking to predict and move ahead of time
  • Containment - A steering force that keeps the character within a zone/area
  • Wander - Random exploration with smooth, natural-looking paths
  • Obstacle Avoidance - A steering force that applies lateral and breaking forces to avoid obstacles
  • Wall Avoidance - A steering force that is applied when characters are in a path to collide with a wall.
  • Path Following - A steering force that keeps characters on a path. The path here is defined by points and splines.
  • Flocking - Set of behaviors that simulates flocks in nature like birds/fish. Group behaviors including separation, alignment, and cohesion
