PS Home Games

Engine: Playstation Home Engine (Lua)

Role: Lead Software Engineer

While at Codename Games, I was the solo engineer working on a small team helping them bring games to the PS Home collection of games.

The first game I worked on was a remake of Cogs.

This was a super fun project with a lot of interesting recursive algorithms as the puzzles started off simple and got fairly complex once the shapes and sizes of the boards increased. Also, having to implement this in lua was quite a challenge.

Later in the lifecycle of the company I also helped push another game out called Clusterpuck.

I wasn't the main programmer on this project and played more of a supporting role apart from driving the original architecture of our codebase. But had to help out a bit more towards the end while shipping this.

A couple other projects that the team worked on that were really cool but sadly didn't end up shipping before PS Home went through a transition are shown below:


Boom! - A cool tower defense game where you played as tanks defending against warships bombarding your island.


SAM RPG - A hex based turn based Roguelike game that was about making hard choices to climb to the top of a mountain as quickly as possible.
